Our History

The journey of growth, expansion, and academic excellence

The establishment of Makueni University College (MUCO) is traced to the origins of South Eastern Kenya University (SEKU) Wote Town Campus, which served as the precursor to the current Constituent College. The Campus commenced operations in March 2012 as one of the four satellite campuses of SEKU, located in Wote Town, Makueni County, approximately 170 kilometres from SEKU’s Main Campus in Kitui.

During its operation, Wote Town Campus functioned within SEKU’s Vision, Mission, Mandate, Strategic Objectives, Core Values, and Philosophy, and was guided by the University Charter, Strategic Plan, and other policy instruments and regulations. It steadily expanded its academic offerings, providing diploma, undergraduate, and postgraduate programmes in social sciences, arts, and science-based disciplines through regular, part-time/weekend, and online modes of study.

By the time of its elevation, the Campus had attained a student population of approximately 750 and had demonstrated academic maturity through the training of three (3) PhD graduates. The Campus also strengthened its mandate through collaborations and partnerships with the Makueni County Government, health institutions, schools, the business community, and national partners.
